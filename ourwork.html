Our Work

Protecting wildlife, restoring forests and empowering communities around Aarey Forest.

Protecting Aarey Forest

AAREY KEY works alongside the Waghoba Habitat Foundation to preserve biodiversity within Mumbai’s Aarey Forest. Through community action, conservation research and restoration initiatives we aim to build a future where wildlife and urban communities coexist sustainably.

Key Initiatives

Habitat Restoration

Rapid urbanization and environmental degradation have significantly impacted the natural ecosystems of Aarey Forest. Our team conducts tree plantation drives, removal of invasive plant species, and restoration of degraded forest areas.

Indigenous Knowledge

Partnering with Adivasi communities to preserve traditional ecological knowledge and sustainable forest practices.

Wildlife Protection

Protecting wildlife is a central mission of our organization. Aarey Forest is home to a diverse range of animals, many of which face threats due to habitat loss and urban expansion. Our team works actively to respond to wildlife emergencies and ensure the safety of injured or displaced animals.

Environmental Education

Environmental education is essential for building a responsible and environmentally aware community. Our organization conducts awareness programs, workshops, and campaigns to educate people about the importance of wildlife conservation and sustainable living.

Community-Led Conservation

AAREY KEY believes that conservation is strongest when local communities actively participate in protecting the ecosystems they depend on.

We collaborate with Adivasi communities, students, and local residents to promote sustainable forest practices, wildlife awareness, and environmental stewardship.

  • Forest clean-up drives and habitat protection
  • Community workshops and environmental education
  • Native tree plantation with local volunteers
  • Promoting coexistence with urban wildlife
